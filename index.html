    Admission Procedure

    Admission Procedure For Undergraduate Program TSLAT

    The T.S. Mishra Law Admission Test is an entrance test conducted by TSM Law school to accept admissions into the T.S Mishra Law School integrated 5-year law program. The structure and pattern of the TSLAT will evaluate candidates on their aptitude in areas like logical reasoning, current affairs, general knowledge & English language proficiency.

    TSLAT balances various aspects essential for success in legal education. It tests a candidate's proficiency in languages, general knowledge, reasoning abilities, and analytical skills—all of which are crucial for future law students.

    The Legacy of TS Mishra Law School

    The late Justice Tribeni Sahai Misra

    An eminent legal personality whose legacy is not unknown. Late Justice T.S. Misra known for his legal acumen and professional knowledge was appointed as the District Government Counsel (D.G.C.) by the State Government in the year 1964 and subsequently was elevated as a Judge of the Allahabad High Court in the year 1971.

    He remained a Senior Judge at Lucknow Bench of Allahabad High Court and went on to become the Chief Justice of Guwahati High Court in 1983.

    Late Justice T. S. Misra also functioned as the Governor of Assam and Meghalaya. He subsequently retired on 14 November 1984. In the year 1986 Late Justice Misra was appointed as the Ombudsman (Lok Ayukta) Bihar.

    Even after having such an outstanding and spectacular career in the legal field Late Justice T.S. Misra always remained an academician and an educationist at heart. His Ideologies reflect deep in the foundation of TSM Law School.

    About Satish Chandra Misra

    Shri Satish Chandra Misra, Senior Advocate and Former Advocate General of Uttar Pradesh was born on 9th November- 1952 at Kanpur. He comes of an eminent family. His father Late Justice T.S. Misra’s legacy is not unknow in the legal field.

    Sri S.C. Misra is blessed with distinguished career in the legal profession. He was appointed Addl. Standing Counsel of Government of India Income Tax Department in the year 1983. Thereafter he was appointed Senior Standing Counsel of Income Tax Department in the year 1986 of High Court, Lucknow Bench, Lucknow and continued as such till he assumed the office as a Rajya Sabha Member.

    The roots of Sh. Satish Chandra Misra reflect through his long-standing career in law and his welfare work. He has been a Chief patron of “All India Blinds Welfare Association”. He has been working for the welfare of Blinds since 1987 when he had filed a P.I.L. for their upliftment in his own name.

    The youngest son of Late Justice T.S. Misra has realised his father’s vision with the establishment of T.S. Mishra University, he is also the Chancellor of the University. Late Justice T.S. Misra was always an academician at heart, and Shri Satish Chandra Misra has given his soul life as TSM School of Law. The vision of Shri Satish Chandra Misra for TSM Law School is to make it the dream law school.

    About TS Mishra Law School

    T .S. Mishra Law School the Dream Law School, was the vision of Late Justice T.S. Misra, whose legacy is not unknown. Even after having such an outstanding and spectacular career in the legal field Late Justice T.S. Misra always remained an academician and an educationist at heart. Which inspired Shri Satish Chandra Misra to establish the T.S. Mishra Law School dedicated to providing quality legal education and prioritising outcome-based education. The vision of TSM School of Law is to promote Law courses, programmes, curriculum, research, collaborations, and interaction through its faculty, infrastructure, and varied opportunities.

    From the academic year 2024-25, TSM Law School offers a five-year Integrated B.B.A. LL.B. and B.A. LLB. programme; one year LL.M. programme (Criminal and Security Law/Constitutional and Administrative Law) and PH.D. (Full Time-Law). The LL.M. programme of TSM School of Law is offered for all law graduates, corporate lawyers, legal practitioners, civil servants, diplomats and judges with two options for specialisation.

    By incorporating new-age learning technologies, the Law school offers competency-based, adaptive, and collaborative education which is also based upon the National Education Policy, 2020. This creates a learning environment that equips students with the skills and knowledge they need to thrive in the evolving legal landscape.

    Student Faq’s

    Frequently Asked Questions!

    What programs does TSM law school offer?
    • 5-Year Integrated LLB (BA LLB, BBA LLB)
    • 3-Year LLB (For graduates with a non-law background)
    • LLM (Master of Laws) with specializations in Constitutional Law, Criminal Law.
    • PhD in Law
    What are the eligibility criteria for the 5-year LLB program?
    • Completed 10+2 or equivalent education with a minimum aggregate score (specific percentage requirements).
    • The candidates require to qualify an entrance exam TSLAT.
    What is the admission process for the 5-year LLB program?
    • Eligibility Check: Ensure you meet the academic and entrance exam requirements.
    • Entrance Exam: Students need to clear an entrance exam TSLAT.
    What are the eligibility criteria for the LLM program?
    • Hold a LLB degree (either 5-year integrated or 3-year) from a recognized university.
    • You must have scored at least 50% in your LL.B. Program (3 years or 5 years).
    How long does it take to complete an LL.M. at TSM Law School?

    The LL.M. program (full-time) is structured for a duration of one year.

    Can I specialize in a particular area of law during the LLM program?
    • Constitutional Law & Administrative Law
    • Criminal Law & Security Law
    Does the TSM Law School provide placement assistance?
    • Campus Recruitment Drives: Connects students with leading law firms, corporates, etc.
    • Internships: Assistance with finding legal internships in top law firms, government agencies, and non-governmental organizations.
    • Workshops: Mock interviews, CV-building, and career counseling.
    Are there any extracurricular activities at the law school?
    • Moot Court Competitions: National and international moot court competitions to develop advocacy skills.
    • Debate Clubs: Regular debates and discussions on legal, political, and social issues.
    • Legal Aid Clinics: Volunteer opportunities where students offer legal assistance to underprivileged communities.
    • Sports and Cultural Events: We host a variety of sports, music, and cultural events throughout the year.
    What career opportunities are available after completing the LLB program?
    • Legal Practice: As a lawyer, advocate, or solicitor.
    • Corporate Sector: In corporate legal departments, compliance, and corporate governance roles.
    • Public Service: As a public prosecutor, legal advisor, or in government law agencies.
    • Judiciary: Prepare for judicial exams to become a judge or magistrate.
    • Academics: Teaching law at universities or conducting legal research.
    Is there a hostel facility available on campus?

    Yes, we offer hostel accommodations for both male and female students. The hostels are equipped with modern amenities, and we have a dedicated team to ensure student welfare.
